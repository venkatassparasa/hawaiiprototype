# TVR/STR Knowledge Base for Chatbot

## Overview
This document contains the comprehensive knowledge base for the TVR/STR (Transient Vacation Rental/Short Term Rental) chatbot, specifically focused on Hawaii County regulations, Ordinance 25-50, and related zoning codes.

## Scope Limitations
- **In Scope**: TVR registration, compliance, enforcement, Ordinance 25-50, Hawaii County Zoning Code Sections 25-4-16 through 25-4-16.3, State laws referenced in Ordinance 25-50
- **Out of Scope**: General County services, non-TVR regulations, broader County-wide questions

## Knowledge Categories

### 1. Registration Process
**Topics Covered:**
- Application process and requirements
- Fee structure ($500 application fee, $250 annual fee, $1,000 NCUC fee, $150+ inspection fees)
- NCUC (Nonconforming Use Certificate) requirements
- Annual inspection requirements
- Timeline and process steps

**Key Information:**
- Registration required for all TVRs operating less than 30 consecutive days
- NCUC required for R-1, R-2, R-3 residential zones
- Annual inspections mandatory for all registered TVRs
- Process timeline: 2-4 weeks (conforming) to 6-10 weeks (NCUC required)

### 2. Compliance Requirements
**Topics Covered:**
- Occupancy limits (2 per bedroom + 2 in R-1, 2 per bedroom + 4 in R-2)
- Parking requirements (1 space per bedroom + 1 additional)
- Noise restrictions (10 PM - 7 AM quiet hours)
- Registration display requirements (12x12 inch sign)
- Annual compliance requirements

**Key Information:**
- Maximum occupancy cannot exceed 12 people regardless of bedroom count
- Children under 12 count as half occupants
- Parking must be on-site and properly surfaced
- Registration number must be prominently displayed

### 3. Enforcement and Penalties
**Topics Covered:**
- Violation types and penalties
- Complaint filing process
- Enforcement procedures
- Appeal process
- Suspension and revocation

**Key Information:**
- Daily fines: $500-$5,000 for operating without registration
- Other violations: $250-$2,000 per offense
- Complaint investigation: 5-10 business days
- Appeal period: 15 days with $250 fee

### 4. Ordinance 25-50
**Topics Covered:**
- Ordinance provisions and requirements
- Relationship to State law (HRS ยง46-7.5)
- Definitions and terms
- Legislative intent and scope

**Key Information:**
- Enacted in 2018 as comprehensive TVR legislation
- References Hawaii Revised Statutes ยง46-7.5
- Defines TVR as rental < 30 consecutive days
- Establishes mandatory registration system

### 5. Zoning Code Sections 25-4-16 through 25-4-16.3
**Topics Covered:**
- Section 25-4-16: TVR definitions and permitted districts
- Section 25-4-16.1: Registration requirements
- Section 25-4-16.2: Parking and development standards
- Section 25-4-16.3: Enforcement procedures

**Key Information:**
- TVRs permitted in R-1, R-2, R-3 (with NCUC), and all commercial districts
- Different requirements for residential vs commercial zones
- Specific parking and development standards by zone type
- Enforcement authority and procedures

### 6. Property-Specific Regulations
**Topics Covered:**
- Residential zones (R-1, R-2, R-3) requirements
- Commercial and resort district regulations
- Agricultural district restrictions
- Special Use Permit requirements

**Key Information:**
- R-1: Single Family, strictest requirements
- R-2: Two Family, moderate requirements
- R-3: Multi-Family, slightly more flexible
- Commercial: Fewer restrictions, follow building codes
- Agricultural: Special Use Permit required

### 7. Process and Timeline
**Topics Covered:**
- Registration timeline
- Renewal process
- Inspection scheduling
- NCUC processing

**Key Information:**
- Registration: 2-4 weeks (conforming) to 6-10 weeks (NCUC)
- Renewal: Annual, 30 days before expiration
- Inspections: Annual, 1-2 weeks to schedule
- NCUC: 4-6 weeks processing time

### 8. Tax Registration Requirements
**Topics Covered:**
- HRS Chapter 237D (Transient Accommodations Tax)
- General Excise Tax (GET) requirements
- Third-party rent collector disclosures (HRS 237D-8.5)
- Federal Employer Identification Number (FEIN)

**Key Information:**
- TAT: 10.25% collected from guests, register within 20 days
- GET: 4% county rate + 0.5% county surcharge on gross income
- Third-party platforms must provide rental data to property owners
- FEIN required for tax registration and business banking

**Resources:**
- HRS Chapter 237D: https://www.capitol.hawaii.gov/hrscurrent/Vol04_Ch0201-0257/HRS0237D/HRS_0237D-.htm
- Tax registration: tax.hawaii.gov

### 9. Property Information and Research
**Topics Covered:**
- Real Property Tax website research
- GIS portal mapping and zoning verification
- Tax Map Key (TMK) identification
- Zoning classification verification

**Key Information:**
- Property ownership, tax map keys, zoning classification available online
- GIS portal provides zoning districts, boundaries, flood zones
- TMK format: 3-4-XXX-XXX-XXX (district-zone-section-plot)
- Zoning verification critical for TVR eligibility determination

**Resources:**
- Real Property Tax website: https://qpublic.schneidercorp.com/Application.aspx?AppID=1048&LayerID=23618&PageTypeID=2&PageID=9876
